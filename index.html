Abstract

In modern organizations, significant energy is wasted when electrical appliances are left running in unoccupied spaces. Our research presents an integrated Smart Energy Management System that monitors device-level consumption using IoT sensors (ACS712, PIR, RCWL) and provides AI-driven optimization strategies.

The system pairs a Python REST API backend and MongoDB database with a cross-platform Flutter mobile application, enabling facility managers to monitor energy consumption, receive occupancy-based alerts, and reduce operational costs and carbon footprints — all from their smartphone.

System Highlights

IoT Energy Monitoring

ACS712 current sensors measure per-appliance power consumption in real time via ESP8266 Wi-Fi microcontrollers.

Occupancy Detection

Dual PIR + RCWL microwave radar fusion on ESP32 delivers high-accuracy, lighting-independent presence sensing.

Flutter Mobile App

A cross-platform Android & iOS app for real-time energy dashboards, occupancy alerts, and device control.

AI Recommendations

Intelligent engine detects wasteful scenarios — room vacant, appliance on — and auto-triggers corrective actions.

Python REST API

Scalable backend processes sensor payloads, validates data, and serves the Flutter app and web dashboard.

MongoDB Database

Flexible NoSQL document store handles time-series IoT data with fast queries for historical energy analytics.

Analytics Dashboard

Web-based visualizations of per-device consumption trends, occupancy patterns, and estimated cost savings.

Sustainable Impact

Proven reduction in unnecessary energy waste, contributing to lower carbon footprints and green operations.
